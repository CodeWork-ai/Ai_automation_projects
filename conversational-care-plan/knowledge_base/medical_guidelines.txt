# [[ GOAL: Acne and Pimples ]]
# STRATEGY: Reduce inflammation, manage sebum production, and support skin barrier health through targeted nutrition and lifestyle adjustments.
# KEY NUTRIENTS: Zinc (anti-inflammatory), Vitamin A (regulates skin cell turnover), Omega-3 Fatty Acids (reduces inflammation), Probiotics (gut-skin axis).
# ACTIONABLE PLAN - DIET:
# - Reduce High-Glycemic Foods: Limit sugar, white bread, and processed snacks. High blood sugar can increase inflammation and sebum.
# - Consider Limiting Dairy: For some individuals, dairy can be a trigger. Try cutting back for 2-3 weeks to observe effects.
# - Incorporate Anti-Inflammatory Foods: Eat more salmon, walnuts, flaxseeds (for Omega-3s) and colorful vegetables.
# ACTIONABLE PLAN - LIFESTYLE:
# - Skincare Basics: Use a gentle cleanser and a non-comedogenic moisturizer. Introduce a product with Salicylic Acid (BHA) 2-3 times a week to exfoliate pores.
# - Do Not Touch Your Face: Avoid picking or squeezing pimples to prevent scarring and spreading bacteria.
# - Clean Pillowcases: Change your pillowcase every 2-3 days.
# SHOPPING LIST ITEMS: Salmon, walnuts, spinach, kale, berries, quinoa, green tea, zinc supplement, salicylic acid cleanser.

# [[ GOAL: Sustainable Weight Loss ]]
# STRATEGY: Create a consistent but moderate calorie deficit through nutrient-dense foods and increased physical activity. The focus is on long-term habits, not a crash diet.
# KEY NUTRIENTS: Protein (for satiety and muscle preservation), Fiber (for fullness), Healthy Fats (for hormone regulation).
# ACTIONABLE PLAN - DIET:
# - Protein Pacing: Aim for 20-30g of protein with each meal. This keeps you full. Example: Greek yogurt for breakfast, chicken salad for lunch, fish for dinner.
# - Calorie Deficit Target: Aim for a modest deficit of 300-500 calories per day from your maintenance level.
# - Meal Composition: Use the Plate Model (1/2 veggies, 1/4 protein, 1/4 complex carbs).
# ACTIONABLE PLAN - EXERCISE:
# - Cardio: 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week (e.g., 30 mins of brisk walking, 5 days/week).
# - Strength Training: 2-3 full-body strength sessions per week. Building muscle boosts your resting metabolism.
# SHOPPING LIST ITEMS: Chicken breast, tofu, eggs, Greek yogurt, broccoli, sweet potatoes, brown rice, almonds, avocados, oatmeal.

# [[ GOAL: Fading Hyperpigmentation ]]
# STRATEGY: Protect the skin from further UV damage while encouraging skin cell turnover and inhibiting melanin production with targeted ingredients.
# KEY NUTRIENTS: Vitamin C (antioxidant, brightener), Vitamin E (protects skin), Niacinamide (reduces pigment transfer).
# ACTIONABLE PLAN - LIFESTYLE & SKINCARE:
# - SUNSCREEN IS NON-NEGOTIABLE: Use a broad-spectrum SPF 30-50 sunscreen every single day, even when indoors or on cloudy days. Reapply every 2 hours if outside. This is the most important step.
# - Morning Routine: After cleansing, apply a Vitamin C serum before your moisturizer and sunscreen.
# - Evening Routine: Introduce a Retinoid or Niacinamide serum a few nights a week to increase cell turnover. Start slowly to avoid irritation.
# - Gentle Exfoliation: Use a gentle chemical exfoliant (like Glycolic or Lactic Acid) once a week.
# SHOPPING LIST ITEMS: Broad-spectrum SPF 50 sunscreen, Vitamin C serum, Niacinamide serum, gentle cleanser, moisturizer.

# [[ GOAL: Managing Menstrual Discomfort (PMS) ]]
# STRATEGY: Balance hormones and reduce inflammation through targeted nutrition and gentle lifestyle adjustments.
# KEY NUTRIENTS: Magnesium (can reduce cramps and mood swings), Iron (to replenish what's lost), Omega-3s (anti-inflammatory), B-Vitamins (for energy).
# ACTIONABLE PLAN - DIET:
# - Increase Magnesium: Eat more dark leafy greens, nuts, seeds, and dark chocolate.
# - Boost Iron: During and after your period, focus on iron-rich foods like lean red meat, lentils, and spinach. Pair with Vitamin C for better absorption.
# - Healthy Fats: Incorporate flaxseeds and fatty fish to help manage inflammation-related pain.
# - Reduce Bloating: Lower your intake of salt and highly processed foods in the week leading up to your period.
# ACTIONABLE PLAN - LIFESTYLE:
# - Gentle Movement: Light exercise like walking, yoga, or stretching can help alleviate cramps.
# - Heat Therapy: Use a heating pad on your lower abdomen to relax muscles.
# - Stress Management: Practice mindfulness or deep breathing, as stress can worsen PMS symptoms.
# SHOPPING LIST ITEMS: Spinach, almonds, dark chocolate (70%+), salmon, lentils, ginger tea, magnesium supplement.
